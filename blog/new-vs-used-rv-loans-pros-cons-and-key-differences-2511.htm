New vs. Used RV Loans: Pros, Cons, and Key Differences

  • Home
  • Blog
  • Blog Details

The dream of hitting the open road in a recreational vehicle (RV) is more appealing than ever. With remote work becoming mainstream and a growing desire for sustainable travel, RVs offer freedom, flexibility, and adventure. But before you embark on your journey, one critical decision awaits: Should you finance a new or used RV?

This guide breaks down the pros, cons, and key differences between new and used RV loans, helping you make an informed choice that aligns with your budget, lifestyle, and financial goals.

The Rising Popularity of RV Ownership

The RV industry has seen a surge in demand, fueled by post-pandemic wanderlust and the shift toward nomadic living. Whether you're a digital nomad, a retiree exploring the country, or a family seeking affordable vacations, an RV can be a smart investment. However, financing is a major consideration—especially with rising interest rates and economic uncertainty.

New RV Loans: The Upsides and Downsides

Advantages of Financing a New RV

1. Lower Interest Rates
Lenders typically offer better interest rates for new RVs because they’re considered lower-risk investments. New models have the latest features, warranties, and no prior wear and tear, making them more attractive to banks and credit unions.

2. Full Manufacturer Warranty
A new RV comes with a comprehensive warranty, covering major systems like the engine, plumbing, and electrical components. This can save you thousands in unexpected repairs during the first few years of ownership.

3. Latest Technology and Features
From solar-powered systems to smart home integrations, new RVs boast cutting-edge amenities that enhance comfort and efficiency. If you prioritize modern conveniences, a new model may be worth the higher upfront cost.

4. Customization Options
Buying new often allows you to choose floor plans, finishes, and add-ons tailored to your needs.

Drawbacks of New RV Loans

1. Higher Depreciation
RVs lose value quickly, much like cars. A new RV can depreciate by 20-30% in the first year alone. If you plan to sell or trade in the future, this could be a financial setback.

2. Larger Loan Amounts
New RVs are expensive, often costing six figures for high-end models. This means higher monthly payments and longer loan terms (sometimes up to 20 years).

3. Insurance Costs
Insuring a new RV is pricier due to its higher value and replacement costs.

Used RV Loans: What to Consider

Benefits of Buying Used

1. Lower Purchase Price
The most obvious advantage is cost savings. Used RVs can be 30-50% cheaper than new ones, allowing you to get more for your budget.

2. Slower Depreciation
A used RV has already taken the biggest depreciation hit, so its value stabilizes over time. This makes it a smarter choice if resale value matters to you.

3. Shorter Loan Terms
With a lower loan amount, you might secure a shorter repayment period, reducing total interest paid.

4. More Negotiation Power
Private sellers and dealerships are often willing to negotiate on price, especially for older models.

Challenges of Used RV Loans

1. Higher Interest Rates
Lenders view used RVs as higher risk, so interest rates are usually 1-3% higher than for new models.

2. Limited or No Warranty
Unless you purchase an extended warranty, you’ll be responsible for all repairs. Pre-owned RVs may have hidden issues, so a thorough inspection is crucial.

3. Outdated Features
Older models might lack energy-efficient systems or modern tech, which could impact your travel experience.

Key Differences Between New and Used RV Loans

Loan Terms and Eligibility

  • New RVs: Loans often extend up to 20 years, with lower rates and stricter credit requirements.
  • Used RVs: Loan terms are shorter (typically 10-15 years), and lenders may be more flexible with credit scores.

Down Payment Requirements

  • New RVs: Lenders may ask for 10-20% down.
  • Used RVs: Down payments can range from 15-25%, depending on age and condition.

Total Cost of Ownership

While a used RV has a lower sticker price, maintenance and repair costs can add up. New RVs have higher upfront costs but fewer immediate out-of-pocket expenses.

How to Choose the Right Option for You

Assess Your Financial Situation

  • Can you comfortably afford higher monthly payments for a new RV?
  • Do you have savings set aside for potential repairs if buying used?

Consider Your Travel Needs

  • Will you use the RV full-time or occasionally? (Frequent travelers may benefit from a new model’s reliability.)
  • Are you tech-savvy and want the latest gadgets?

Research Lenders and Loan Options

Compare rates from banks, credit unions, and specialized RV lenders. Look for pre-approval to gauge your budget.

Final Thoughts

Whether you choose a new or used RV loan depends on your priorities—financial flexibility, long-term value, or modern comforts. By weighing the pros and cons, you’ll be better equipped to make a decision that fuels your adventures without straining your wallet.

Now, all that’s left is to hit the road and start exploring!
