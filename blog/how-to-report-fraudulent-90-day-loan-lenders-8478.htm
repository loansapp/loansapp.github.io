How to Report Fraudulent 90 Day Loan Lenders

  • Home
  • Blog
  • Blog Details

The rise of predatory lending practices has become a global concern, with fraudulent 90-day loan lenders exploiting vulnerable borrowers. These unscrupulous lenders often target individuals with poor credit scores or urgent financial needs, trapping them in cycles of debt with hidden fees, exorbitant interest rates, and deceptive terms. If you’ve encountered such a lender, knowing how to report them can protect others from falling victim.

Recognizing Fraudulent 90-Day Loan Lenders

Before reporting, it’s crucial to identify red flags that distinguish predatory lenders from legitimate ones.

Common Warning Signs

  • Unrealistically High Interest Rates: If the APR (Annual Percentage Rate) exceeds legal limits (often 36% or higher), it’s a strong indicator of predatory lending.
  • Hidden Fees: Lenders who don’t disclose additional charges upfront may be operating fraudulently.
  • Pressure Tactics: Aggressive marketing, threats, or demands for immediate payment are major red flags.
  • No Physical Address or License: Legitimate lenders are registered and provide verifiable contact information.
  • Requests for Upfront Payments: Demanding fees before releasing funds is illegal in many jurisdictions.

Steps to Report Fraudulent Lenders

Once you’ve identified a suspicious lender, take action immediately. Reporting helps authorities shut down scams and prevent further exploitation.

1. Gather Evidence

Document all interactions with the lender, including:
- Loan agreements and terms
- Emails, text messages, or call logs
- Bank statements showing unauthorized withdrawals
- Any misleading advertisements

2. Contact Your Bank or Credit Card Company

If the lender has already withdrawn funds, notify your financial institution. They can:
- Block further transactions
- Reverse unauthorized charges
- Provide guidance on next steps

3. File a Complaint with Regulatory Agencies

Different countries have agencies dedicated to financial fraud. In the U.S., these include:

Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)

  • Submit a complaint online at www.consumerfinance.gov.
  • The CFPB investigates and may take legal action against fraudulent lenders.

Federal Trade Commission (FTC)

  • Report scams at ReportFraud.ftc.gov.
  • The FTC tracks patterns of fraud and shares data with law enforcement.

State Attorney General’s Office

  • Each state has its own financial regulations. Contact your local AG’s office for state-specific protections.

4. Report to Online Platforms

If the lender operates through social media, payment apps, or websites:
- Google: Report fraudulent ads via Google’s complaint form.
- Facebook/Meta: Flag suspicious loan offers as scams.
- PayPal/Venmo: Dispute unauthorized transactions.

5. Warn Others Through Reviews

Posting honest reviews on platforms like:
- Better Business Bureau (BBB)
- Trustpilot
- Reddit’s r/Scams

can alert potential victims.

Legal Recourse Against Fraudulent Lenders

If you’ve suffered financial harm, consider legal action.

Hiring a Consumer Rights Attorney

A lawyer specializing in financial fraud can:
- Help recover lost funds
- Sue for damages under consumer protection laws
- Assist in class-action lawsuits if multiple victims exist

Small Claims Court

For smaller losses, filing in small claims court may be a cost-effective option.

Protecting Yourself from Future Scams

Prevention is key. Follow these tips to avoid predatory lenders:

  • Check Lender Credentials: Verify licenses through your state’s financial regulator.
  • Read Reviews: Research lenders thoroughly before borrowing.
  • Avoid "Guaranteed Approval" Claims: Legitimate lenders assess creditworthiness.
  • Understand the Terms: Never sign a contract without reading the fine print.

By taking these steps, you not only protect yourself but also contribute to shutting down fraudulent operations that prey on the financially vulnerable.
